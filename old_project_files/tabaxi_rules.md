# Tabaxi Race Rules (D&D 5e)

The Tabaxi is a highly mobile race with excellent utility for stealth, infiltration, and burst movement. While originally released in *Volo's Guide to Monsters*, they were updated in *Monsters of the Multiverse* (MotM) with several mechanical improvements.

---

## Core Traits & Mechanics

### Ability Score Increases
*   **Monsters of the Multiverse (MotM):** Choose one of the following:
    *   +2 to one score and +1 to a different score.
    *   +1 to three different scores.
*   **Original (Volo's):** Fixed +2 Dexterity and +1 Charisma.
*   **2024 Rules Note:** Use the ability score increases from your chosen Background instead of racial traits.

### Age
*   **Lifespan:** Tabaxi possess lifespans equivalent to human beings, typically living between 80 and 100 years.

### Size
*   **Monsters of the Multiverse:** You choose whether you are **Medium** or **Small** when creating the character.
*   **Original (Volo's):** Always **Medium**.
*   *Note:* Medium is generally preferred over Small to avoid being grappled or swallowed whole by many enemies.

### Speed
*   **Walking Speed:** 30 feet.
*   **Climbing Speed:** 30 feet (equal to your walking speed). You can use your claws to climb, and you don't need to spend extra movement for climbing. Climbing speed is extremely useful for vertical positioning, getting out of melee range, and infiltrating.

### Darkvision
*   **Range:** You can see in dim light within **60 feet** as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can’t discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.
*   *Utility:* In games with a lot of dark dungeons, Darkvision is essential.

### Feline Agility
*   **Effect:** When you move on your turn in combat, you can **double your speed** until the end of that turn.
*   **Requirement:** Once you use this trait, you can’t use it again until you move 0 feet on one of your turns.
*   **Utility:** This doesn't require an action or bonus action. The reality of combat in 5e is that you won’t move every turn, and when you do need to move you sometimes need to move a great distance all at once. Feline Agility makes that much easier, serving as a powerful tactical tool for closing gaps or escaping danger.

### Cat’s Claws
*   **Natural Weapon:** Your claws are natural weapons used to make unarmed strikes.
*   **Damage (MotM):** On a hit, you deal **1d6 + Strength modifier** slashing damage.
*   **Damage (Volo's):** On a hit, you deal **1d4 + Strength modifier** slashing damage.
*   **Climbing:** Provides the climbing speed mentioned above.
*   *Utility:* Essentially decorative for combat. Manufactured weapons will almost always be more effective, but the claws grant the crucial climbing speed.

### Cat’s Talent
*   **Proficiencies:** You gain proficiency in two skills: **Perception** and **Stealth**.
*   *Utility:* Two skills are welcome on any character. Perception is one of the most rolled skills in the game, and Stealth strongly supports rogues and scout builds.

### Languages
*   **Monsters of the Multiverse:** You can speak, read, and write **Common** and **one other language** of your choice.
*   **Original (Volo's):** Common and one other language.

---

## Suggested Classes & Synergies
*   **Rogue & Bard:** Exceptional synergy. Two free proficiencies (Perception/Stealth) stack with high given class skills. Feline Agility helps close gaps without burning Cunning Action dashes.
*   **Monk:** Feline Agility doubles the Monk’s already incredible speed. The climbing speed makes them extremely mobile without casting *Fly*. The pre-selected skills align perfectly with Monk ability scores.
*   **Fighter & Barbarian:** The climbing speed and burst movement answer their biggest weakness: reaching ranged/flying enemies and getting into positions without built-in flight/teleportation.
*   **Ranger:** Excellent fit as a scout. Natural synergy with Dexterity, Stealth, and Perception.
*   **Cleric (Trickery):** The free proficiencies essentially make them as skilled as a rogue, and gaining Darkvision for free helps since clerics can't natively cast it.
